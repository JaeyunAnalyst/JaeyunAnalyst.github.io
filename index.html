Excel: Automated Cash Flow Statement Analysis using Macros and VBA

This Excel tool automatically performs data wrangling, analyzes raw data of any cash flow statement, and generates automated cash flow analysis which takes 5 to 7 hours for an accountant to complete.

The purpose of this project is to automate and simplify the process of analyzing cash flow statements, which typically requires hours of manual effort. By leveraging Excel, specifically through VBA (Visual Basic for Applications) and macros, the tool efficiently processes raw financial data and generates automated, accurate cash flow analyses—saving significant time and minimizing human error.

Financial statements such as the income statement and balance sheet include both cash and non-cash elements, like depreciation (a reduction in asset value), amortization (allocation of intangible asset costs), and provisions (reserves for future liabilities). These components are based on estimates and accounting assumptions, which can sometimes obscure a true picture of a company’s financial situation.

In contrast, the cash flow statement strictly reports actual cash transactions that reflect the movement of money in and out of the business. Unlike other financial statements, it does not include non-cash adjustments or estimates, making it a more objective and reliable measure of a company’s liquidity and cash flow.
